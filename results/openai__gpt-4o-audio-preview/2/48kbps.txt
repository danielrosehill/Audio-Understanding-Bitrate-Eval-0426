Her soup was different every week, a little bit of this and that. Sometimes she’d toss in leftover chicken bones, or maybe just a single carrot sliced thinly. I remember how she’d say you didn’t need a recipe—just a good memory and a bit of patience. The bowls were chipped and worn, but we loved them just the same. None of us cared; the warmth in those bowls could fix any kind of bad day.
